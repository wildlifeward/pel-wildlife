Medications

Studies in pigeons show that they have an increased number of kappa opiod receptors (compared to mammals that have more mu receptors). As such, birds dont respond to mu agonists (morphine, buprenorphine, fentanyl) in the same way mammals do. Butorphanol (Kappa agonist - a good sedative but not good pain control in mammals) works better in birds than it does mammals for pain control. Study in parrots in 2006 by Sladky et al showed liposome encapsulated butorphanol tartrate with SC admin provided analgesia and detectable serum concentrations in parrots for up to 5 days.

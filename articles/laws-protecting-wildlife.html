Laws Protecting Wildlife

Posted on Fri 10 May 2019 by Wildlife Ward

Posted in Laws

Laws in action

  • Migratory Bird Treaty Act of 1916/1918
    • Makes it illegal to take, possess, import, export, transport, sell, purchase, barter, or offer for sale, any migratory bird, or the parts, nests or eggs of such a bird except under the terms of a valid federal permit
    • Originally an agreement between the US and Canada, with influence from Great Britain
    • Three species are not covered: rock doves(pigeons), starlings, house sparrows
  • Eagle protection act (1940)
    • Gave eagles additional protection under the migratory bird treaty act
  • Endangered Species Act 1973/1988
  • Wild Bird Conservation Act 1992
  • States have their own regulations in addition to the above, make sure you know the laws of your state to maintain appropriate permits to treat animals.
  • Note: Banding permits are available for all species except vultures because they defecate on their legs to cool themselves and this poses a health hazard to the bird if a band is on its leg

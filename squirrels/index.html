Squirrels

Squirrels

  • Orphans

    • Make sure the baby has actually been orphaned. If baby has just fallen out of the tree and isnt injured or seen near a cat, place in a warm box and put it at the base of the tree. Can try to rub the squirrel to make it 'scream'. Put the squirrel in the box and walk at least 30 feet away. Getting out of view is best so mom (if shes around) will feel comfortable enough to come down and get the baby and take it back to the nest. Leave baby alone for 1-2 hours. if this time has lapsed, baby may indeed be orphaned.

  • Feeding

    • Rehydration solution: 1 tsp salt, 3 tsp sugar, 4 cups luke warm water --> This will make an ok homemade rehydration solution in a pinch. Bare in mind that pedialyte is much better to use.
    • Esbilac is ok to use, add whipping cream to it (5-25ml/cup). start at 5ml and increase cream. if loose stool develops, decrease cream. For solid esbilac powder; 2 parts water: 1 part esbilac powder + 1/4 part heavy whipping cream. though fox valley formula is better.
    • Feed squirrels about 5-7% of body weight.

  • Husbandry

    • Use Tea Tree oil topically on penis to prevent penis suckling by other squirrels

  • Medication

    • Streptomycin and procaine penicillin toxic to rats and mice and probably are for squirrels
    • Cat bites:
      • older than 5ish weeks: clavamox 20mg/kg, metacam 0.2 mg/kg loading then 0.1mg/kg maintenance
      • young squirrels: TMS
    • Internal parasites: Albon

  • Aging

    • Eastern Gray Squirrel. Weights vary drastically depending on geographic location. More northern living gray squirrels weigh more than southern living gray squirrels.
    • Weeks old Weight (g) CC's/feeding or day Length Description
      Birth to 1 week ? 0.75-1.25cc q 2.5 hours including overnight 3" excluding tail Pink, no fur, eyees closed tightly. Cannot thermoregulate, must be stimulated to urinate/defacate
      2 weeks ? 2.5-3cc q 3 hours (6 feedings). no more overnight feedings 3" or so Pink, scant fuzz or fur, no fur on belly, eyes closed
      3 weeks ? 4-5.5cc q 3 hours (6 feedings) 4" excluding tail Thin fur all over, eyes closed, lower incisors erupt between 19-21 days.
      4 weeks ? 6-9cc q 3.5 hours (5 feedings). start giving solid food 5" long excluding tail Fur all over, eyes open at 27-30 days. Will begin trying to eat solid food. still needs milk replacer
      5 weeks ? 10-12cc q 4 hours (4 feedings). start giving drinking water 6" long excluding tail Fur all over, thinner fur on underside. upper incisors erupt between 4-6 weeks old. Not curling tail yet.
      6 weeks ? 12-14cc q 4 hours (4 feedings). add striped sunflowers and other fruits to feed Eyes fully open, cheek teeth erupt at ~ 6 weeks old. Can sit up, can curl tail.
      7-8 weeks ? start weaning. Solid food should consist of 50% rodent blocks and 50% other fruits, nuts, veggies. Give as much formula as they want within reason. 7w old: formula 3x/day. 8w old: formula 2x/day 7-8" excluding tail. Furry everywhere including underside of tail. Tail looks bushier. Can sit up and hold food in front paws. Sleeping less, more active
      8-12 weeks ? weaning typically complete by 10 weeks. usually will self-wean but can continue to give small amount of formula to them if they want it. 3/4 of adult size by 12-13 weeks. Long fur, bushy tail, increasingly active, climb very well. mini adults.. Prep squirrels for release at 8 weeks. release by 12-13 weeks.
      12+ weeks ? Regular squirrel food. rodent blocks, grapes, avocado, nuts, banana, broccoli, squash, etc ? Provide food and water for post-release squirrels for at least 4 weeks.
